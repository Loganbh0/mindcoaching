The Masters 2025

The Masters Tournament is one of golf’s most iconic and prestigious championships, held each spring at Augusta National Golf Club in Augusta, Georgia. Since its first event in 1934, the Masters has stood as a celebration of tradition, excellence, and sportsmanship — offering unforgettable moments on one of the world’s most beautifully designed courses.

The 2025 Masters once again brought together the finest golfers from around the globe to compete for the coveted Green Jacket. From the pressure of Amen Corner to the final putt on the 18th green, this year’s tournament delivered incredible drama, inspiring comebacks, and outstanding displays of skill.

This website is your hub for everything you need to know about the 2025 Masters. Here you’ll find comprehensive coverage of the tournament, including player stats, daily highlights, and exclusive statements straight from the competitors themselves. Whether you missed a round or want a deeper look at your favorite player’s performance, our site brings you closer to all the action at Augusta.

We also want to offer our heartfelt congratulations to the 2025 Masters Champion, Rory Mcllroy. Their performance throughout the tournament was a true display of precision, focus, and resilience — earning them the honor of slipping on the Green Jacket and etching their name into golf history with the accomplishment of the Grand Slam.

Thank you for joining us in celebrating another unforgettable Masters Tournament. Be sure to explore the site for post-tournament analysis, player interviews, and more as we reflect on another extraordinary year at Augusta National.

The Playoff Round

Here is a video of the final playoff round in the 2025 Masters. Rory McIlroy and Justin Rose both hit incredible tee shots, showcasing their skill throughout the round. After a thrilling back-and-forth, McIlroy triumphed, ending a decade-long drought to capture his first Masters victory.


What has Rory said about his win?

  • Rory McIlroy, on Winning the Masters: “My dreams have been made today.”
  • McIlroy, on His Emotions When the Final Putt Dropped: “It was all relief. There wasn't much joy in that reaction. It was all relief.”
  • McIlroy, on Never Giving Up Despite So Many Close Calls: “I'm not going to compare it to life moments like a marriage or having a child. But it's the best day of my golfing life. I’m very proud of myself. I'm proud of never giving up. I'm proud of how I kept coming back and dusting myself off and not letting the disappointments really get to me.”
  • McIlroy, on Hearing Past Champions Say He Was Going to Win the Masters: “It was a heavy weight to carry and, thankfully, now I don't have to carry it.”
  • McIlroy, on What Happens Now That He Won the Masters: “What are we all going to talk about next year?”

What have others said?

  1. Justin Rose, on Nearly Pulling Off an Epic Comeback: “So it's the kind of putt you dream about as a kid. To have it and hole it, it was a special feeling. And unfortunately, the playoff, they always end so quickly. That’s sudden death. You don't really get an opportunity. If you're not the guy to hit the great shot or hole the great putt, it's over.”
    • Rose made 10 birdies, including one on the 18th hole to finish off a final-round 66. He lost to McIlroy on the first hole of the playoff.
  2. Bryson DeChambeau, on the Atmosphere on Sunday: “Electric. I loved it.”
    • DeChambeau was in the final pairing with McIlroy on Sunday. He started the day two shots behind, but struggled with 3-over 75 in the final round.
  3. Scottie Scheffler, on Getting in Contention: “I was just proud of the way we hung in there and put up a good fight. A few different things happen today and I maybe have a chance in the tournament. I wasn't far off.”
    • The defending champion started the day seven shots behind McIlroy, but he got close down the stretch with a 3-under 69.
  4. Patrick Reed, on Holing Out for Eagle on No. 17: “To make that was really cool because you sit there and go, hey, mathematically I might have a chance.”
    • The 2018 champion shot 3-under 69 and finished in third place.
  5. Min Woo Lee, on His Last Hole of This Masters: “It was actually a funny story. I didn't chip in all week, and on 17 I hit it in the front bunker and I said to my caddie, ‘I'm either going to chip this in or I'm going to miss the last green on purpose and chip in.’”
    • Lee admitted he didn’t miss the 18th green on purpose, but he did end up in the right bunker – and knocked it in.
